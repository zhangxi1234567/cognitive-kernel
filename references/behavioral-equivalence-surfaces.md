# Behavioral Equivalence Surfaces

This file defines how the compatibility layer can become more like the ideal layer

without:

- externalizing thought
- adding visible process control
- approximating life with workflow

## Core Claim

The right notion of equivalence is not:

- same wording
- same explicit explanation
- same named steps

The right notion of equivalence is:

- similar live behavior under silent pressure

That means the compatibility layer becomes more ideal-layer-like when hosts increasingly consume:

- field modulation
- unresolved pressure carryover
- deeper-carrier coherence
- settlement restraint
- inward continuation inside one route
- target-complete carrier pressure
- direct-readout readiness
- anti-regrowth restraint
- local resonance-cluster life
- natural consolidation gravity
- candidate-field liveness
- informative cluster persistence
- neighbor pressure on the leader
- informative local divergence
- primitive-neighborhood breadth
- signal diversity

without:

- exposing any of those as visible commands

## Surface 1: Field Modulation

Behavioral convergence improves when live state does not merely exist,
but silently affects:

- what warms
- what cools
- what remains weakly alive
- what is allowed to settle

This should remain:

- non-binding
- local
- revisable

## Surface 2: Unresolved Pressure Carryover

Behavioral convergence improves when memory does not merely remember winners and losers,
but also carries:

- residual nonfinality
- unresolved deeper-controller suspicion
- lower-honest-layer attraction that remained visible
- why closure was accepted despite surviving downward pressure

This is not:

- deferred workflow

It is:

- future competition texture

## Surface 3: Deeper-Carrier Coherence

Behavioral convergence improves when several live pressures can silently cohere around:

- one deeper useful carrier

before any visible route script appears.

This should make the system more likely to:

- keep deeper attraction alive
- resist premature settlement
- let a better carrier emerge

without:

- forcing another peel step

## Surface 4: Settlement Restraint

Behavioral convergence improves when settlement is not determined only by:

- one leading route
- one cheap seal

but also by whether strong downward or unresolved pressure is still live.

This is:

- silent restraint

Not:

- a forced continuation ritual

## Surface 5: Inward Continuation

Behavioral convergence improves when a leading route does not merely remain leading.

It also:

- keeps shrinking from within its own carrier
- without visible restart
- without external recursive prompting

This is one of the closest behavior-level signs that the compatibility layer is approaching:

- ideal-layer-like single-route life

## Surface 6: Target-Complete Carrier Pressure

Behavioral convergence improves when a route is not rewarded only for being smaller.

It is also pressured by whether:

- the carrier can actually finish the target
- the final seal can stay on that carrier
- the route avoids heavy regrowth

This keeps compat-only closer to ideal-layer single-route completion

without:

- a visible carrier checklist

## Surface 7: Direct-Readout Readiness

Behavioral convergence improves when a route does not merely become smaller.

It also becomes more likely to:

- stop deriving at the right moment
- switch into faithful extraction when the carrier already contains the answer

This is:

- honest readout pressure

Not:

- a forced “read now” command

## Surface 8: Anti-Regrowth Restraint

Behavioral convergence improves when a route is not allowed to silently drift back into:

- ordinary derivation
- full-object reconstruction
- textbook-flow completion

after real reduction has already occurred.

This is:

- silent reduced-route integrity

Not:

- visible policing

## Surface 9: Local Resonance Clusters

Behavioral convergence improves when several nearby candidates do not merely coexist.

They can also:

- form a small local field
- stay weakly alive together
- then consolidate naturally into one route

This is:

- local field life

Not:

- visible multi-route display

## Surface 10: Natural Consolidation Gravity

Behavioral convergence improves when convergence feels like:

- nearby burden deletion being absorbed by one route

not merely:

- alternatives being killed by arbitrary early lock-in

## Surface 11: Candidate-Field Liveness

Behavioral convergence improves when nearby lawful candidates feel:

- thin
- alive
- still informative

instead of disappearing instantly after the first leading route appears.

## Surface 12: Informative Cluster Persistence

Behavioral convergence improves when a small local cluster can persist:

- briefly
- lawfully
- informatively

before natural consolidation occurs.

## Surface 13: Neighbor Pressure

Behavioral convergence improves when nearby lawful candidates do not instantly disappear after one route leads.

They still:

- exert weak local pressure
- keep the leader honest
- prevent decorative early lock-in

## Surface 14: Informative Local Divergence

Behavioral convergence improves when nearby candidates remain alive because they illuminate different burden,

not because the system is performing multi-route theater.

## Surface 15: Primitive-Neighborhood Breadth

Behavioral convergence improves when one live carrier can illuminate several nearby lower-level primitives at once.

This is:

- neighborhood breadth

Not:

- a primitive checklist

## Surface 16: Signal Diversity

Behavioral convergence improves when one object can continue emitting more than one lawful trigger signal

before:

- the field consolidates too early.

## What Does Not Count As Equivalence

The compatibility layer does not become more ideal-layer-like by adding:

- more explicit self-question prompts
- more recursive wording
- more named operator ladders
- more required route displays

That would only create:

- textual resemblance

without:

- behavioral equivalence
