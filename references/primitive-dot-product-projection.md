# Primitive: Dot Product By Projection

## Pattern

A dot-product range or comparison problem is really a projection problem.

## Trigger Signal

- dot product appears
- moving point or moving vector
- range / max / min / sign of dot product
- geometry picture is easier than raw vector multiplication
- one fixed direction or one projection axis seems to organize the problem

## Level-Agnostic Core

Do not treat the dot product as opaque algebra if one projection picture already reveals the target.

## Control Mechanism

The move works because:

- dot product is projection times length
- or it can be rewritten into a short length / angle / energy identity
- one projected component often controls range, sign, or extremum behavior

## Preserved Structure

Typical preserved items:

- projected length onto the target direction
- sign of alignment / opposition
- range control by endpoint or constrained projection
- same target value after rewriting into projection language

## Earliest Honest Layer

This is not first a component-multiplication problem.
It is a "shadow on one direction" problem.

## Fast Move

1. Ask what is being projected onto what.
2. Replace the dot product by projection or a short identity.
3. Use geometric endpoint control rather than brute component expansion.

## Compression Move

Typical burden deletion:

- bilinear algebra -> projection picture
- full component expansion -> one directional readout
- moving vector system -> endpoint / shadow control

## Level Transfer

- early level: shadow length or direction picture
- university level: projection, orthogonality, and norm decomposition
- research level: energy pairing, bilinear form readout, or dominant projected mode

## Cross-Domain Analogue

- physics: work/flux-like quantity reduced to one projected component
- debugging: many metrics reduced to one key aligned signal
- strategy: complex option reduced to one directional score

## Where It Transfers

- vector range questions
- orthogonality checks
- projection geometry
- optimization with one fixed direction
- bilinear or energy readout problems

## Failure Mode

- projection picture alone does not pin the endpoint geometry
- multiple moving parts still need a minimal range skeleton
- the rewritten identity is true but the controlling direction was chosen badly

## Verification Bill

- state the projection meaning explicitly
- if using a range argument, identify which endpoints or constraints control the projection
