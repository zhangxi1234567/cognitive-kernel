# Primitive: Function Archetype Matching

## Pattern

An abstract or ugly function is easier once it is matched to a known behavior family instead of being treated as a brand-new object.

## Trigger Signal

- the formula or relation looks unfamiliar but the behavior signature feels familiar
- additive, multiplicative, periodic, odd/even, or transformed structure suggests a known family
- the problem is really about behavior, not exact local algebra
- one mother function or archetype would immediately organize monotonicity, symmetry, zeros, or extrema
- the raw expression is noisy, but a prototype family is visibly nearby

## Level-Agnostic Core

Do not treat every new function as new.
Ask what known family is wearing these clothes.

## Control Mechanism

The move works because:

- the function inherits behavior from a mother family
- the functional signature compresses many local details into one recognizable archetype
- once the archetype is called, symmetry, monotonicity, periodicity, or growth behavior become cheaper to read

## Preserved Structure

Typical preserved items:

- behavior signature
- target-relevant monotonicity / parity / periodicity
- map from transformed form back to the mother family
- admissibility of the family match

## Earliest Honest Layer

Before manipulating the function locally, ask:

- what old function does this behave like?
- what family would explain this pattern fastest?
- what becomes free once the mother family is identified?

## Fast Move

1. Read the function or relation as a behavior signature.
2. Match it to one or more plausible archetype families.
3. Use anchors, special values, or legality checks to confirm or eliminate the candidates.
4. Solve through the confirmed family behavior instead of raw local clutter.

## Compression Move

Typical burden deletion:

- novel formula -> mother function family
- local algebra chase -> family behavior readout
- many possible behaviors -> one archetype-guided route
- raw noise -> inherited structure

## Level Transfer

- low-complexity form: this ugly thing is really that old familiar function
- compact formal form: transformed function inherits a mother-pattern behavior law
- advanced formal form: classify into an archetype class, normal family, or canonical behavior regime

## Cross-Domain Analogue

- debugging: match a messy failure to a known bug family
- strategy: route a messy market or scenario into a known strategic archetype
- science: classify a complicated signal into a known regime or model family before fitting every detail

## Where It Transfers

- abstract-function problems
- transformed function graph questions
- hidden periodicity or symmetry families
- over-standard / mother-function routing
- any problem where family identity kills more work than local manipulation

## Failure Mode

- the family resemblance is cosmetic only
- a key property needed for the target is not inherited
- the solver jumps from similarity to equality without locking the match
- the chosen archetype organizes part of the behavior but not the asked target

## Verification Bill

State:

- what family is being called
- what feature triggered the match
- what property is inherited legally
- what anchor or check confirms the match

## Seed Examples

- additive relation -> linear family
- multiplicative-on-addition relation -> exponential family
- product/sum-angle style relation -> trigonometric family
- symmetry/shift relation -> periodic or parity-based family
