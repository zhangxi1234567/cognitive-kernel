# Wu Boshi Conic Eccentricity Clusters

This file extracts stable route clusters from the conic-eccentricity teaching material visible in the merged notes.

## Core Principle

The eccentricity of a conic is not just a formula target.
It often becomes readable once the conic is reduced to one controlling triangle, ratio, or definition picture.

## Extracted Clusters

### 1. Horizontal / Vertical Orientation Split

Use when:

- a conic parameter may flip the major axis or transverse axis direction

Move:

- classify horizontal vs vertical first
- then read the eccentricity formula in the correct orientation

### 2. Special-Triangle Readout

Use when:

- the geometry closes into a familiar integer or radical triangle

Move:

- identify the controlling triangle
- use the remembered ratio directly

Examples from the notes:

- `3,4,5`-style closures for eccentricity quick readout

### 3. Definition Trigger

Use when:

- the problem gives a direct conic-definition relation
- distance-to-focus or distance-to-directrix structure is visible

Move:

- use the definition first, not the standard coordinate grind

### 4. Rectangle / Diagonal Property

Use when:

- lines through the origin cut a conic and the resulting quadrilateral has diagonal or midpoint structure

Move:

- replace point-chasing with the midpoint / rectangle relation

### 5. Cosine-Theorem Focal Triangle Route

Use when:

- the controlling picture is a non-right focal triangle

Move:

- abandon brute coordinates
- treat the focal triangle as the real object
- use the cosine theorem as the shortest reliable seal

## Hard Rule

In eccentricity problems, do not default to symbol expansion before asking:

- what is the controlling triangle?
- what orientation branch am I in?
- what definition or midpoint structure is already visible?
