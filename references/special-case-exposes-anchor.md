# Wu Boshi Special Case Exposes Anchor

This file defines why special cases often reveal fixed points, fixed lines, and fixed values.

Its purpose is:

- explain why special positions are not random shortcuts
- treat special cases as anchor revealers

## Core Rule

When a family is moving, ask:

- which special position removes the most slack?
- which special case forces contact with the hidden anchor?

Then compress:

- moving family -> anchor-revealing case

## Bottom-Layer Logic

- ordinary positions hide the fixed controller behind too many freedoms
- special cases remove freedoms
- once the slack is gone, the family is forced to touch the anchor

This is why:

- tangency
- overlap
- vertical / horizontal
- endpoint
- coincident points

often expose:

- fixed point
- fixed line
- fixed value
- invariant relation

## What This Explains

- special-position guesses in conics
- fixed-point discovery
- choosing extreme slopes
- choosing merged points or tangent cases

## Trigger Signals

- family of lines / chords / points
- fixed-point or fixed-line suspicion
- parameter family with a likely constant controller
- proofs where generic motion is too cluttered

## Failure Boundary

Do not overfire this move when:

- the special case changes the mechanism instead of revealing it
- the family does not actually preserve the anchor outside the special case
- the route stays local and cannot map back

## Transfer

- school math: conic and geometry fixed-point questions
- university math: revealing cases for invariant sets
- research-style transfer: critical regime reveals bottleneck / anchor mechanism
