# Primitive: Container To Section

## Universal Primitive Note

This primitive is one instance of a broader reduction:

- high-dimensional object -> controlling slice / projection / subspace

It should transfer beyond elementary geometry whenever one reduced view controls the decisive relation.

## Pattern

A symmetric high-dimensional packing or tangency problem is actually controlled by one lower-dimensional slice.

## Trigger Signal

- 3D packing or contact geometry
- equal spheres / cylinders / cones / rotational symmetry
- the decisive distances lie in one obvious symmetry plane
- the full solid feels noisy, but a cross-section feels clean

## Control Mechanism

The move works because:

- the decisive centers and contact directions are preserved in one section
- the section keeps the target-relevant distances or tangency relations
- the symmetry plane carries the same control logic as the full solid

## Preserved Structure

Typical preserved items:

- center-to-center geometry
- target-relevant tangency relations
- support distances and section radii
- order and feasibility relations visible in the controlling slice

## Earliest Honest Layer

If the 3D object is really ruled by one side view, stop carrying all of 3D in your head.
Cut to the slice that still contains the control geometry.

## Fast Move

1. Identify the section that contains the decisive centers / tangency directions.
2. Flatten the problem into that section.
3. Solve the 2D distance or tangency relation there.

## Compression Move

Typical burden deletion:

- 3D solid -> 2D section
- full packing configuration -> center geometry in one plane
- curved contact mess -> simple tangency picture

## Where It Transfers

- spheres in cylinders or boxes
- cone / cylinder / ball tangency
- shortest distance in symmetric solids
- any high-dimensional geometric problem controlled by one slice

## Failure Mode

- the chosen section is not actually controlling
- asymmetry leaves a hidden degree of freedom outside the slice
- the target depends on 3D relations not preserved in the section

## Verification Bill

- state why the decisive centers and contacts all lie in the chosen section
- state what is preserved when cutting
- if needed, verify that no off-section configuration can do better
