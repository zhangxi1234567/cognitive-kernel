# Wu Boshi Tangency As Boundary Revelation

This file defines tangency as a special case of boundary-revelation logic.

Its purpose is:

- treat tangency as a mechanism revealer, not only as a geometric fact
- use tangent / coincidence cases to expose fixed controllers
- unify many `切线 / 切点` routes under one deeper principle

## Core Rule

When a family can move until two objects just touch, ask:

- what becomes zero at tangency?
- what relation becomes sharp or unique?
- what hidden fixed object becomes visible only at the touching boundary?

Then compress:

- moving family -> tangent boundary case

## Trigger Signals

- tangent / touching / coincidence wording
- discriminant zero in algebraic shell
- chord length goes to zero
- family of lines or circles approaching a limit position

## Compression Moves

- interior family -> boundary case
- many intersections -> unique contact relation
- variable chord -> tangent point
- moving object -> fixed support line or fixed point

## Why It Works

- tangency is a seam where the family stops having slack
- touching often reveals the controlling equality
- what is hidden in the interior becomes explicit at the boundary

## Good Uses

- conics
- circles and envelopes
- fixed-point / fixed-line questions
- extremum and limiting-position geometry

## Failure Boundary

Do not overfire this move when:

- tangency is decorative and not controlling
- the target depends on generic interior behavior
- the touching case destroys rather than reveals the needed structure

## Transfer

- school math: circle and conic tangent questions
- university math: discriminant-zero boundary and envelope logic
- research-style transfer: instability onset / critical touching regime
