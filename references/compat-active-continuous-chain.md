# Compat-Active Continuous Chain

This file defines the compatibility-active form of continuous dimensional-reduction chaining.

Its purpose is:

- make compat-only execution more able to keep shrinking inside one route
- preserve chain-like inward continuity without prescribing a visible reduction ladder

## Core Claim

Compat-only execution should already allow:

- one reduction to expose a smaller carrier
- that carrier to keep attracting further lawful shrinking
- the route to approach direct readout without visible restart

It should not stop too easily at:

- one good middle carrier
- one good local rewrite
- one elegant but still thick representation

## What This Means In Compatibility Form

At the compatibility-active level, continuous chain behavior means:

- a leading route can keep improving from within its own carrier
- later reductions need not appear as unrelated route restarts
- the route is biased toward direct readout and cheapest seal once the carrier is strong enough

This is not:

- a requirement to perform N reductions
- a mandatory “compress again” ritual

## Allowed Compatibility Pressure

The compatibility layer may already preserve:

- `inward_continuation_pressure`
- `deeper_carrier_coherence`
- `lowest_honest_layer_attraction`
- `target_complete_readiness`

These are enough to support:

- weak chain continuity

without:

- a visible descent script

## What Must Stay Forbidden

Do not turn continuous chaining into:

- “reduce once more”
- “find the next layer”
- a numbered shrink sequence
