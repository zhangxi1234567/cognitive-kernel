# Wu Boshi Underconstrained Completion

This file defines the move:

- when the visible conditions are fewer than the full object would normally require, test the most symmetric or most standard completion first

## Core Principle

Some problems are intentionally underdescribed on the surface.

Instead of panicking, ask:

- what standard completion would make the structure click?
- what symmetric completion is most likely to expose the hidden intended route?

This is not blind guessing.
It is a search heuristic for underconstrained shells.

## Common First Completions

- equilateral or isosceles triangle
- 30-60-90 or 45-45-90 completion
- tangent / coincidence case
- equal-parameter or equal-length case
- symmetric or centered placement

## Why It Works

Many exam and training problems hide the intended route in the most symmetric lawful completion.

## Hard Rule

A completion is only useful if:

- it satisfies the given conditions
- it exposes a controlling structure
- it can later be justified or used as a route guide rather than mere wishful thinking
