# Wu Boshi Research Control Laws

This file translates the foundational control laws into research-native reasoning.

Its purpose is:

- make the project explicitly usable beyond mathematics
- show how the same deep laws become research actions rather than math-flavored explanations
- prevent "research transfer" from staying rhetorical

## Core Rule

Research use should not be:

- math analogy pasted on top of science vocabulary

Research use should be:

- the same control laws regenerated as:
  - mechanism selection
  - readout selection
  - perturbation design
  - candidate-space compression
  - cheapest discriminating experiment

## The Research Translation Of The Foundational Laws

### 1. Controller Selection Law -> Mechanism Selection

Research question:

- which mechanism actually controls the observed outcome?

Operational forms:

- identify the rate-limiting step
- identify the bottleneck pathway
- identify the one state transition that explains the phenotype
- identify the one readout that tracks the live regime

Bad version:

- describe every visible biological object equally

Good version:

- rank mechanisms by control weight

### 2. Fake-Degree Elimination Law -> Effective-Variable Compression

Research question:

- which variables are shell-level, redundant, or downstream shadows of the same hidden controller?

Operational forms:

- latent-factor compression
- merge equivalent conditions
- collapse correlated biomarkers into one effective axis
- quotient out nuisance variation

Goal:

- many measurements -> few effective state variables

### 3. Carrier Rewrite Law -> Research Readout Rewrite

Research question:

- what lower carrier makes this system easier to reason about?

Operational forms:

- mechanistic story -> state transition graph
- omics matrix -> latent state/readout axis
- pathway diagram -> control-flow structure
- phenotype cloud -> one discriminating assay

Goal:

- replace storytelling burden with a measurable carrier

### 4. Boundary Revelation Law -> Critical-Regime Discovery

Research question:

- where does the system lose slack?

Operational forms:

- threshold crossing
- failure onset
- saturation
- collapse regime
- switching point between two phenotypes

Goal:

- use critical regimes to reveal the hidden mechanism faster than average-case observation

### 5. Probe Law -> Perturbation Design

Research question:

- what is the cheapest perturbation that most strongly stresses the live mechanism?

Operational forms:

- one knockout / knockdown
- one drug perturbation
- one media / temperature / stress change
- one minimal intervention to separate candidate mechanisms

Goal:

- do not measure everything first
- probe the mechanism sharply

### 6. Static Skeleton Law -> State Model Extraction

Research question:

- what static structure underlies the observed dynamics?

Operational forms:

- state-transition graph
- attractor / stable-state map
- regime diagram
- causal skeleton

Goal:

- turn evolving behavior into a compact structure that can be reasoned about

### 7. Balance Law -> Equilibrium / Neutral-Bias Identification

Research question:

- where does directional bias disappear?

Operational forms:

- equilibrium state
- neutral balance point
- symmetric competition state
- homeostatic regime

Goal:

- identify where competing effects balance and the system changes character

### 8. Anchor Exposure Law -> Fixed Reference Discovery

Research question:

- what fixed anchor survives the family of conditions?

Operational forms:

- invariant baseline
- fixed biomarker relation
- fixed reference state
- conserved bottleneck

Goal:

- stop tracking every condition separately
- read the anchor that organizes them all

### 9. Candidate-Space Compression Law -> Hypothesis Ranking

Research question:

- how can the hypothesis space be made much smaller before large experiments?

Operational forms:

- eliminate impossible mechanisms early
- cluster hypotheses by shared predictions
- rank explanations by discriminative value
- collapse broad hypothesis sets to a few live worlds

Goal:

- avoid full-space blind search

### 10. Readout Law -> Direct Mechanism Readout

Research question:

- once compressed enough, what can now be directly read instead of inferred through a large model?

Operational forms:

- direct biomarker readout
- direct regime call
- direct upstream/downstream call
- direct response-class assignment

Goal:

- read what the reduced system is already saying

### 11. Minimum Honest Seal Law -> Cheapest Discriminating Experiment

Research question:

- what is the minimum experiment or analysis that can still kill the wrong route?

Operational forms:

- one assay that separates live mechanisms
- one critical perturbation
- one stress test at the decisive regime
- one minimal causal check

Goal:

- do not run a whole campaign when one sharp experiment can collapse the space

### 12. Low-Floor Ownership Law -> Cross-Expertise Executability

Research question:

- after reduction, can a weaker but competent scientific reasoner still own the next decisive step?

Operational forms:

- does the reduced explanation only sound elegant?
- or can a non-specialist now choose the next assay, variable, or mechanism test correctly?

Goal:

- real research reduction should increase cross-expertise executability, not just narrative elegance

## Research Workflow From These Laws

For a research problem, the preferred order is:

1. identify the target phenomenon
2. choose the likely controller
3. compress fake degrees of freedom
4. rewrite the problem into a measurable carrier
5. find the boundary / critical regime
6. design the cheapest perturbation probe
7. extract the static skeleton
8. compress the candidate mechanism space
9. read the direct implication if possible
10. choose the cheapest discriminating experiment
11. check whether a weaker solver could still carry the route

## Example Carrier Rewrites

- cancer phenotype -> state-transition problem
- heterogeneous omics features -> latent control axis
- drug response panel -> perturbation discrimination matrix
- pathway story -> bottleneck control graph
- cell-fate dynamics -> attractor map

## Hard Rule

Research transfer is not complete unless all three are visible:

1. the foundational control law
2. the carrier rewrite
3. the minimum discriminating experimental or analytical seal
