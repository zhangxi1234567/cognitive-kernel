# Compat-Active Fundamental Microclusters

This file defines the compatibility-active form of several lower-level primitive constellations.

Its purpose is:

- make compat-only execution more likely to weakly light up small nearby primitive constellations
- preserve low-level combinability without turning it into a forced primitive menu

## Core Claim

Compat-only execution should already allow one live structure to weakly suggest microclusters such as:

- boundary + limit
- special value + symmetry
- normalization + balance
- direct comparison + readout

These are not:

- required combinations
- ordered steps

They are:

- nearby low-level constellations

Interpretation rule:

- microclusters are local resonance residue
- not low-level scripts or mandatory pairings

## What This Means In Compatibility Form

At the compatibility-active level, fundamental microclusters mean:

- a carrier may illuminate more than one nearby primitive family at once
- those nearby families may stay weakly linked for a short time
- eventual consolidation may absorb only part of that cluster while letting the rest cool

This helps compat-only feel less like:

- one favorite trick

and more like:

- a small live primitive neighborhood

## Allowed Compatibility Pressure

The compatibility layer may already preserve:

- `fundamental_microcluster_visibility`
- `primitive_neighborhood_breadth`
- `signal_diversity`

These are enough to support:

- weak lower-level microcluster life

without:

- primitive choreography

## What Must Stay Forbidden

Do not turn microclusters into:

- “always check boundary then limit”
- “always try symmetry with special values”
- a low-level primitive checklist

## Final Rule

Compat-only execution should already allow small lower-level primitive constellations to weakly appear.

It must not expose them as a mandatory combination table.
