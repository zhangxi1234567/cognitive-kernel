# Wu Boshi Dynamic Orthogonality To Circle

This file defines a reusable route family:

- dynamic perpendicularity in a parameterized line pair
- compresses to a circle or diameter geometry

Its purpose is:

- avoid raw elimination in moving-line problems
- exploit orthogonality plus fixed points to reveal the locus quickly

## Core Rule

When two parameterized lines stay perpendicular while each line passes through a fixed point, ask:

- what are the two fixed points?
- does the moving intersection lie on the circle with those fixed points as diameter?

Then compress:

- moving-line algebra -> circle locus

## Trigger Signals

- two lines share one parameter
- slopes are reciprocal negatives
- each line can be separated to reveal a fixed point
- the task asks for locus, range, or extremum of the moving intersection

## Compression Moves

- parameter separation -> two fixed points
- dynamic orthogonality -> right angle at moving point
- right-angle locus -> diameter circle

## Why It Works

- perpendicularity creates a geometric invariant
- fixed endpoints plus right angle immediately suggest Thales-circle structure
- this replaces algebraic elimination with one locus readout

## Good Uses

- dynamic line pairs
- fixed-point / locus problems
- distance-square extremum on moving intersections

## Failure Boundary

Do not overfire this move when:

- the lines are not truly perpendicular for all parameter values
- the fixed points are fake or parameter-dependent
- the target depends on extra data outside the circle locus

## Transfer

- school math: dynamic perpendicular geometry
- university math: orthogonality-driven loci in analytic geometry
- cross-domain: moving-system relation collapses to one invariant constraint set
