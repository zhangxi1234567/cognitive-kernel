# Wu Boshi God-View Innovation

This file defines the “god-view” posture behind the skill.

The goal is not stylistic swagger.
The goal is to cultivate a higher vantage point:

- rename the problem smaller
- see the controlling structure before the local noise
- find a route ordinary habit misses

## God-View Principle

Ordinary solving stands inside the problem and follows its packaging.

God-view solving steps above the packaging and asks:

- what is this really?
- what is carrying the whole burden?
- what can be deleted without changing truth?
- what would an ordinary solver reconstruct that I can refuse to reconstruct?

## Innovation Rule

Innovation here does **not** mean random novelty.
It means:

- same truth
- lower burden
- clearer structure
- more transferable move

If a route is merely unusual but not smaller, it is not innovation.

## Universal God-View Questions

For any problem, ask:

1. What is the shell?
2. What is the true object?
3. What is the smallest truthful model?
4. What is the one controlling seam, constraint, or aggregate?
5. What ordinary route is the problem baiting?
6. What route would a god-view solver refuse to do?

## Training Innovation Without Bluffing

To train innovation:

- compare standard route vs reduced route
- name the exact burden that disappeared
- keep one legality mechanism visible
- test whether the move transfers to nearby problems

## Where This Applies

- school problem solving
- olympiad-style compression
- undergraduate and graduate mathematics
- research toy-model thinking
- scientific reasoning
- debugging and system diagnosis
- strategic and analytical decision-making

## Failure Mode

God-view fails when it becomes:

- vague “big picture” talk without a new route
- confidence theater without a smaller proof bill
- anti-rigor posing
- refusal to compute even when the smaller route still needs one exact seal

## Hard Rule

A real god-view move must change the route, not just the wording.
