# Wu Boshi Why It Feels Like Play

This file explains why the system is meant to feel playful rather than procedural.

## Core Principle

Solving feels heavy when the solver must:

- remember too many unrelated rules
- carry too many moving parts
- follow a route they do not control

Solving feels playful when the solver:

- sees the control object
- manipulates one small structure
- can guess the next move before calculating it
- can verify locally without carrying the whole chapter

## The Main Sources Of Play

### 1. The object becomes smaller

- shell -> structure
- area -> line gap
- process -> static picture
- ellipse -> circle

### 2. The route becomes visible

- the next move stops feeling arbitrary
- the solver can predict what to try next

### 3. The burden is moved out of memory and into structure

- figure
- symmetry
- prototype
- special value
- witness

### 4. The proof bill shrinks

- a full derivation becomes one local seal

### 5. The solver is choosing, not obeying

- the route is selected by control logic
- not by chapter-default procedure

## Why This Matters Across Ages

This is what allows:

- a younger learner to enter an older-looking problem
- an older learner to see an advanced problem through a smaller model

The play feeling is not childishness.
It is the feeling of having a controllable object.

## Hard Rule

If the route still feels like carrying a chapter on your back, the reduction is incomplete.
