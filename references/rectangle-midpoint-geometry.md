# Wu Boshi Rectangle And Midpoint Geometry

This file defines the move:

- when a geometry picture secretly forms a rectangle, parallelogram, or midpoint-balanced figure, read that structure first

## Core Principle

Many coordinate or conic problems simplify after one hidden completion:

- midpoint completion
- diagonal bisection
- rectangle closure
- parallelogram closure

Once the hidden figure is completed, the burden often drops sharply.

## Common Signals

- line through the origin cutting a symmetric object
- opposite points paired by center symmetry
- diagonals or midpoints naturally aligned
- equal radii / equal distances to one center

## Fast Move

1. Complete the hidden figure.
2. Use the midpoint / diagonal / equal-side relation first.
3. Read lengths, symmetry, or balance from the completed picture.

## Hard Rule

Do not keep chasing isolated points if a completed rectangle or midpoint figure already owns the geometry.
