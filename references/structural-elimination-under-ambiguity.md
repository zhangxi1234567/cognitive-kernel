# Wu Boshi Structural Elimination Under Ambiguity

This file defines how to proceed when a problem does not feel fully determined at first glance.

Its purpose is:

- avoid freezing when perfect certainty is not available immediately
- eliminate impossible structures first
- use structural reasonableness to shrink the candidate set before full closure

## Core Rule

When a problem feels underdetermined or noisy at first, do not stop.

Ask:

- what candidates are already impossible?
- what structures survive all obvious constraints?
- what remains after fast elimination?

Then compress:

- broad uncertainty -> narrow live candidate set

## Trigger Signals

- multiple-choice items with incomplete immediate closure
- new-style questions with many plausible paths
- partial information that still rules out many worlds
- “I cannot solve yet, but many options already look structurally dead”

## Compression Moves

- full solve -> eliminate impossible candidates first
- perfect certainty demand -> bounded structural certainty
- large option space -> small live set

## Why It Works

- many hard questions do not need total certainty at the first move
- early elimination often exposes the real route
- structural reasonableness can be lawful if it is tied to visible constraints

## Good Uses

- multiple choice
- parameter screening
- answer-shape pressure
- fast benchmark settings

## Failure Boundary

Do not overfire this move when:

- elimination is based only on vibe
- no real structural constraints support the pruning
- the remaining candidates are still too unconstrained to trust

## Transfer

- school math: option elimination and bounded-answer screening
- research-style transfer: shrink hypothesis space before full testing
- decision work: rule out impossible worlds before optimizing among live ones
